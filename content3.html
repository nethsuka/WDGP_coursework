Conservation of Marine Biodiversity

Threatened Species


Marine ecosystems are home to iconic yet endangered species like whales, sea turtles, sharks, and coral reefs. These species face threats such as habitat degradation, pollution, overfishing, and climate change. Conservation efforts focus on protecting their habitats, reducing human impacts, and raising awareness to ensure their survival and the health of marine ecosystems.


Habitat Preservation


Preserving diverse marine habitats like coral reefs, mangroves, seagrass beds, and polar ecosystems is vital for ecosystem health and resilience. These habitats provide critical functions such as shoreline protection, carbon sequestration, and nursery grounds for marine species. Conservation efforts target threats such as habitat destruction, pollution, and climate change to safeguard these essential ecosystems.


Marine Protected Areas (MPAs)


Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) are designated zones where human activities are regulated to conserve marine biodiversity. MPAs enhance fisheries, protect critical habitats, and promote ecosystem resilience. However, their effectiveness depends on factors such as proper design, enforcement, and stakeholder engagement. Conserving MPAs is crucial for safeguarding marine biodiversity and ensuring sustainable ocean management.
